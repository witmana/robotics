Marlborough Robotics Program

Mission Statement

The Marlborough Robotics Program seeks to empower young women and inspire them to pursue careers in Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math (STEM). This mission is pursued through participation in the FIRST Technical Challenge, which requires students to design, build, and program a robot to compete with other teams in an annual game. Throughout this challenge an emphasis is placed on the engineering design process, collaborative problem solving, and the safe utilization of a wide variety of tools and technology. The mission extends beyond Marlborough through student led outreach and extensive collaboration with the local engineering community.

Program Learning Goals for Students

  • Utilize a design process to solve a complex problem
  • Employ quantitative analysis to make decisions and improve outcomes
  • Work together as a team to design, build, and program a robot to compete in the FTC game
  • Gain an understanding and appreciation of engineering skills and their broad applications
  • Visually articulate ideas using sketches, multiview drawings, and computer aided design
  • Develop a working knowledge of parts and mechanisms common to FTC robotics
  • Learn the applications and safe operation of all tools located in the SPARC space
  • Share knowledge and passion for STEM through extensive outreach

Associated Learning Targets and Gradebook Code

Design Process

Utilize a design process to solve a complex problem

  • DP.1 - Breakdown a complex task into a sequence of specific robot actions (ex. flowchart)
  • DP.2 - Identify a specific robot action and define its functional requirements with enough precision to facilitate a successful solution
  • DP.3 - Research an existing solution for a functional requirement, apply it to a current game task, and evaluate its likelihood of success
  • DP.4 - Perform a cost-benefit analysis and prioritize the completion of game tasks or robot features
  • DP.5 - Use an illustrative method to compare and contrast competing solutions (ex. C-sketch)
  • DP.6 - Develop the solution to a problem by considering analysis opportunities, risks, and countermeasures (ex. FRDPARRC Chart)

Quantitative Analysis

Employ quantitative analysis to make decisions and improve outcomes

  • QA.1 - Design a test, experiment, or prototype and use it to collect quantitative data that can be used to make decisions about a robot design or improve robot performance.
  • QA.2 - Use a quantitative, systematic process to evaluate competing design solutions and determine their ability to meet the functional requirements/criteria of the problem (i.e. Pugh Chart).
  • QA.3 - Carry out a mathematical calculation to guide the design of a robot mechanism. (ex. Torque calculation, power calc)
  • QA.4 - Distinguish between shear, compressive, and tensile forces and suggest strategies to withstand them in a part or assembly

FIRST Tech Challenge

Work together as a team to design, build, and program a robot to compete in the FTC game

  • FTC.1 - Clearly explain the annual FTC game, including play periods, scoring options, and penalties.
  • FTC.2 - Name the judged awards available at each tournament, describe their criteria, and explain how the judges assess them.
  • *FTC.3 - Chart tying team performance to judged awards
  • *FTC.4 - Mini essay on Gracious Professionalism

Engineering Skills

Gain an understanding and appreciation for engineering skills and their broad real world applications

  • ES.1 - Create an engineering notebook entry that documents a problem and progress towards its solution with clear text, photos, captions, and quantitative analysis.
  • ES.2 - Provide specific, justified feedback on a design so it can be improved over successive iterations.
  • ES.3 - Thoughtfully and objectively consider ideas and evaluate  potential solutions from a variety of perspectives. (ex. six hats)
  • ES.4 - Systematically identify and troubleshoot problems and appreciate the value of learning from failures
  • ES.5 - Describe the impact of robotics in a given industry, citing specific examples of how robots are used to solve a real world problem.
  • ES.6 - Analyze a solution based on a fundamental principle such as the KISS principle, Newton’s Laws, Conservation of Energy etc.

Visual Communication

Visually articulate ideas and solutions using sketches, multiview drawings, and computer aided design

  • VC.1 - Create a visual representation of a concept with a level of detail appropriate for the stage of the design process
  • VC.2 - Sketch a multi-view drawing of a robot part or mechanism with enough detail to clearly indicate critical dimensions, function, and means of construction.
  • VC.3 - Create an Onshape assembly of a robot subsystem with sufficient precision to facilitate construction and anticipate its functionality
  • VC.4 - Create an Onshape model of a custom part for fabrication on a laser cutter or 3D printer
  • VC.5 - Articulate a portion of the team’s journey by creating a visually engaging page for the Engineering Portfolio.

Parts and Mechanisms

Gain an understanding of parts and mechanisms common to FTC robotics and their real world counterparts.

  • PM.1 - Distinguish between ‘metric’ and ‘standard’ parts systems and quickly estimate conversions between metric and standard sizes.
  • PM.2 - Identify and describe the use of common robot structural components including channels, extrusions, brackets, hubs, clamps, mounts, spacers, plates, bars, angle beams, attachment blocks, standoffs and tubing.
  • PM.3 - Identify and describe the use of common robot motion components including gears, sprockets, chain, pulleys, belts, linear slides, bushings, bearings, axles, racks and leadscrews.
  • PM.4 - Identify and describe the use of common robot fastening hardware including button head screws, socket head cap screws, hex head screws, lock nuts, kep nuts, and set screws.
  • PM.5 - Identify and describe the function of common mechanisms in FTC robotics including lifts, intakes, and manipulators.

Tools and Safety

Learn the applications and safe operation of all tools located in the SPARC space

  • TS.1 - Recite and consistently follow shop safety procedures including appropriate use of safety glasses, tying back hair and loose clothing, clamping work materials, and being aware of self and others
  • TS.2 - Choose the appropriate tool to tighten a fastener and secure it firmly while avoiding stripping the head, threads, or receptacle.
  • TS.3 - Describe the proper first aid or emergency procedures given the following situations; minor scrape or cut, burns of any degree, serious lacerations, pinches, crushing injuries, or fire.

Outreach

Share knowledge and passion for STEM through extensive outreach

  • OR.1 - Verbally describe a mechanism on the robot, its function, its underlying design process and the quantitative analysis that led to its creation.  
  • OR.2 - Identify and describe the relationships between subsystems on a robot.
  • OR.3 - Design a marketing material for the team such as a poster, banner, social media post, cover page, button, or sponsor packet
  • OR.4 - Interact with a team at a tournament, learn the specifics of a unique mechanism or feature of their robot, and share it with the rest of the team.
  • *OR.5 - Craft and send a professional email to a potential mentor, collaborator, sponsor, or outreach audience.
